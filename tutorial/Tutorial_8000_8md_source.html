1 Introduction {#intro}
2 =====================
3 
4 This tutorial presents various tools and techniques developed in
5 the [Statistical Machine Translation](http://divf.eng.cam.ac.uk/smt) group at
6 the [Cambridge University Engineering Department](http://eng.cam.ac.uk).
7 
8 The tutorial is intended to serve as a guide for the use of the tools, but our research publications contain the
9 best descriptions of the algorithms and modelling techniques described here.
10 The most relevant publications for this tutorial are listed below (\ref Refs). In
11 particular, this tutorial is based on the Russian-English SMT system
12 developed for the WMT 2013 evaluation - we suggest reading the system description [\ref Pino2013] before starting on the tutorial.
13 
14 Our complete publications can be found at <http://divf.eng.cam.ac.uk/smt/Main/SmtPapers>.
15 
16 HiFST grew out of the Ph.D. thesis work of Gonzalo Iglesias.

30 \section intro_features Features Included in this Release
31 
32 - HiFST -- Hierarchical phrase-based statistical machine translation system based on \ref OpenFst
33 - Direct production of translation lattices as Weighted Finite State Automata
34 - Efficient WFSA rescoring procedures
35 - \ref OpenFst wrappers for direct inclusion of \ref KenLM and ARPA language models as WFSAs
36 - Lattice Minimum Bayes Risk decoding
37 - Lattice Minimum Error Rate training
38 - Tutorial for Hiero translation using Recursive Transition Networks and Pushdown Transducers
39 - Client/Server mode
40 - Shallow-N translation grammars
41 - Source-sentence `chopping' procedures
42 - WFSA true-casing
43 - ...
