1 RTNs and PDAs {#pda}
2 =====
3 
4 As already discussed with respect to \ref lpruning, HiFST generates an
5 initial representation of the space of translation in the form of an
6 RTN, which is then transformed either to WFSAs or to PDAs using the
7 \ref OpenFst
8 [Replace](http://openfst.org/twiki/bin/view/FST/ReplaceDoc) operation
9 prior to application of the language model. This leads to two
10 alternative translation pipelines starting from the RTNs (see Figure 2
11 \ref Allauzen2014). Both piplines start with RTNs containing
12 translation grammar scores alone, however in the HiPDT pipeline, WFSAs
13 are not created until after the LM is applied to the translations:
14 
15  - HiFST: Translation via WFSAs
16  - RTNs are transformed to WFSAs by the OpenFst [fstreplace](http://openfst.org/twiki/bin/view/FST/ReplaceDoc) operation
17  - WFSAs are intersected with LMs
18  - The WFSA is pruned under the combined translation grammar and LM scores
19  - HiPDT: Translation via PDTs
20  - RTNs are transformed to PDAs via the OpenFst [pdtreplace](http://www.openfst.org/twiki/bin/view/FST/FstExtensions) operation
21  - PDAs are intersected with the LMs
22  - WFSAs are generated from PDAs via pruned Expansion
23  - The WFSA is further pruned under the combined translation grammar and LM scores
24 
25 In HiPDT, the RTN at the top-most cell of the CYK grid is converted
26 into a PDA (via the Replace operation in the OpenFst Extensions) and
27 efficiently composed with a language model to produce another PDA. To
28 obtain the final output FSA, the PDA is expanded to an FSA either
29 entirely (if memory is sufficient and the language model is small
30 enough) or via pruned expansion (for larger language models). This is
31 useful in exploring large and complex translation grammars where HiFST
32 requires a lot of local pruning. However, it requires the language
33 model to be 'small' (for example, entropy-pruned as in [\ref
34 Iglesias2011, \ref Allauzen2014]). Therefore, we then typically
35 rescore the pruned output FSA with a stronger language model.
36 
37 Both the HiFST and the HiPDT pipelines are implemented by the HiFST program, and are run here with the Shallow-1 translation grammar and the 4-gram language model:
38 
39  > hifst.${TGTBINMK}.bin --config=configs/CF.baseline
40  > hifst.${TGTBINMK}.bin --config=configs/CF.baseline.pdt
41 
42 Translation via PDTs uses more memory (as described in \ref Allauzen2014), both otherwise the outputs should be identical:
43 
44  > printstrings.${TGTBINMK}.bin --semiring=lexstdarc -u --input=output/exp.baseline/LATS/1.fst.gz --label-map=wmaps/wmt13.en.wmap -w
45  ...
46  <s> parliament does not support the amendment , which gives you the freedom of tymoshenko </s> 42.6999,-19.4512
47 
48  > printstrings.${TGTBINMK}.bin --semiring=lexstdarc -u --input=output/exp.baseline.pdt/LATS/1.fst.gz --label-map=wmaps/wmt13.en.wmap -w
49  ...
50  <s> parliament does not support the amendment , which gives you the freedom of tymoshenko </s> 42.6999,-19.4512

58 \section pda_rtns Generating RTNs
59 
60 Translation can be re-run, but with added instructions to save the RTNs to disk:
61 
62  > hifst.${TGTBINMK}.bin --config=configs/CF.baseline --hifst.writertn=output/exp.baseline/rtn/?/%%rtn_label%%.fst --grammar.storentorder=output/exp.baseline/rtn/ntmap --hifst.rtnopt=yes &> log/log.baseline.rtn
63 
64 The RTNs are written to the directory `output/exp.baseline/rtn/*` as
65 
66  > ls output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/
67  1001000009.fst 1003002003.fst 1003003002.fst 1003005001.fst 1003006001.fst 1004001001.fst 1004003000.fst 1004004001.fst 1004006000.fst
68  1003002001.fst 1003002004.fst 1003004001.fst 1003005002.fst 1003007001.fst 1004002000.fst 1004003001.fst 1004005000.fst 1004007000.fst
69  1003002002.fst 1003003001.fst 1003004002.fst 1003005003.fst 1004001000.fst 1004002001.fst 1004004000.fst 1004005001.fst 1004008000.fst
70 
71 and the non-terminal mapping is written to output/exp.baseline/rtn/ntmap :
72 
73  > cat output/exp.baseline/rtn/ntmap
74  S 1
75  D 2
76  X 3
77  V 4
78 
79 Each RTN name is of the form 1ABC.fst , where A, B, and C are 3-digit strings.
80  - A is the numerical code for a non-terminal in the grammar; in this case, 001 corresponds to S.
81  - B indicates a position in the source sentence: 0 <= B < I, where I is the source sentence length
82  - C indicates the offset to the end of a span, 0 <= C and B+C < I
83  - The automata 1ABC.fst corresponds to T_(A,B,B+C) in the formulation of \ref lpruning
84 
85 In this example, the automata 1003002002.fst contains all derivations headed by X, the third non-terminal, and spanning source positions 2 to 4 (=2+2).
86 
87 The root automata is 1001000009.fst, since A=001 (the S non-terminal),
88 B=0 (the first source position), and C=9 (the sentence has 10 words).
89 
90 The automata is a representation of all possible translations of the
91 source sentence under this grammar, as can be seen by printing its paths (here the first 5):
92 
93  > printstrings.${TGTBINMK}.bin --semiring=lexstdarc -u --nbest=5 --input=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/1001000009.fst
94  ...
95  1 1004001000 135 20 103 1004004000 1004005000 1004006000 1004007000 1004008000 2
96  1 1004001000 135 20 103 1004004000 1004005000 1003006001 1004008000 2
97  1 1004001000 135 20 103 1004004000 1003005001 1004007000 1004008000 2
98  1 1004001000 135 20 103 1003004001 1004006000 1004007000 1004008000 2
99  1 1004001000 135 20 103 1004004000 1004005000 1004006000 1003007001 2
100  ...
101 
102 As can be seen, the symbols are a mix of target language symbols (1,135,20,102,2,...) and pointers to other automata (1004004000, 1004005000, ...).
103 Conversion of the RTN is done by recursive substitution of these symbols by the FSTs to which they point, starting from the root automata.

105 \section pda_rtn_expansion Translation by RTN Expansion to WFSAs
106 
107 The `fstreplace` command is used to expand the collection of WFSAs comprising the RTN into a single equivalent WFSA.
108 
109  > fstreplace | head -n 3
110  Recursively replaces FST arcs with other FST(s).
111 
112  Usage: fstreplace root.fst rootlabel [rule1.fst label1 ...] [out.fst]
113 
114 Note that the root FST and root label are always of the form 1001000C, where C = I - 1 , where I is the source sentence length.
115 HiFST uses the same names for rules and labels, so we get get the filenames and labels in the right form by, e.g.
116 
117  > ls output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/1*.fst | sed 's,\(.*\)/\(.*\).fst,\1/\2.fst \2,' > tmp/script.replace
118  > head -2 tmp/script.replace
119  output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/1001000009.fst 1001000009
120  output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/1003002001.fst 1003002001
121 
122 The following will expand the RTN into an FSA:
123 
124  > fstreplace `cat tmp/script.replace` > output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/T.fst
125 
126 The WFSA `output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/T.fst` is the replacement of the RTN that was generated in translation. It is a transducer: the input maintains the pointers to the automata used in translating each source span; the output is the corresponding translation

146 \subsection rtn_lm_app Applying the LM to the WFSA
147 
148 The `applylm` tool can be used to apply the baseline 4-gram language model to T via composition. This generates a new WFSA containing both grammar and language model scores:
149 
150 - Input
151  - M/interp.4g.arpa.newstest2012.tune.corenlp.ru.idx.withoptions.mmap : n-gram LM
152  - output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/T.fst : WFSA containing translation grammar scores
153 - Output
154  - output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG.fst : WFSA containing translation grammar and LM scores
155 
156 The output is written in the form of a transducer, with the RTN labels as the input symbols and the target language words on the output symbols:
157 
158  > applylm.${TGTBINMK}.bin --lm.load=M/interp.4g.arpa.newstest2012.tune.corenlp.ru.idx.withoptions.mmap --semiring=lexstdarc --lattice.load=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/T.fst --lattice.store=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG.fst
159  > fstshortestpath output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG.fst | fstrmepsilon | fsttopsort | fstprint | head

161  1 2 1004001000 0

186 \section pda_pda_expand Translation by PDA Pruned Expansion
187 
188 (Note, as of July 2015: The OpenFST PDT command line operations do not work well with our lexicographic library, so the standard tropical semirring must be used in this example. This makes the example slightly more complex than it actually is in practice.)
189 
190 **Step 0.** Dump the RTN under the full translation grammar, as described in \ref pda_rtns
191 
192  > hifst.${TGTBINMK}.bin --config=configs/CF.baseline --hifst.writertn=output/exp.baseline/rtn/?/%%rtn_label%%.fst --grammar.storentorder=output/exp.baseline/rtn/ntmap --hifst.rtnopt=yes &> log/log.baseline.rtn
193 
194 **Note** that that translations under the grammar `G/rules.shallow.gz` and LM `M/interp.4g.arpa.newstest2012.tune.corenlp.ru.idx.withoptions.mmap` are written to the FST `output/exp.baseline/LATS/1.fst.gz` in the usual way. However the RTN is written to the directory `output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/` *without* the language model score.
195 
196 **Step 2.** Use `lexmap` to transform the RTN files `output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/100*.fst` into in the standard tropical semiring (`output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/100*.fst-tp`):
197 
198  > for f in output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/100*.fst; do lexmap.${TGTBINMK}.bin --action=lex2std --input=$f > $f-tp; done;
199 
200 **Step 3.** Transform the RTN into a PDT:
201 
202  > ls output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/1*.fst-tp | sed 's,\(.*\)/\(.*\).fst-tp,\1/\2.fst-tp \2,' > output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/script.replace
203  > pdtreplace --pdt_parentheses=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/parens.txt `cat output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/script.replace` > output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/T.pdt
204 
205 where the `pdt_parentheses' option indicates that the open/close parentheses symbols are to be stored into a file; note that this file varies with the translations. This is used later in order to expand the PDA to an FSA.
206 
207 **Step 4.** Compose the PDA with the weak language model. This is done via the standard composition algorithm, but making sure that open/close parentheses symbols are treated as epsilons by the composition algorithm. To accomplish this, their respective output symbols need to be relabelled to 0 before applying the LM:
208 
209  # relabel
210  > tr '\t' '\n' < output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/parens.txt | sed 's/$/\t0/' > output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/parens-to-epsilon.txt
211  > fstrelabel -relabel_opairs=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1//parens-to-epsilon.txt output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/T.pdt > output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/Tb.pdt
212  # apply LMs
213  > applylm.${TGTBINMK}.bin --lm.load=M/interp.4g.arpa.newstest2012.tune.corenlp.ru.idx.withoptions.mmap --lattice.load=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/Tb.pdt --lattice.store=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG.pdt
214 
215 **Step 5.** Expand the resulting PDT into an FSA while applying a pruning weight of 9:
216 
217  > fstproject output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG.pdt | pdtexpand --pdt_parentheses=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/parens.txt --weight=9 > output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG.fst
218 
219 The translation result following pruned expansion of the PDT should agree with the translation hypothesis produced directly by HiFST:
220 
221  > printstrings.${TGTBINMK}.bin --semiring=lexstdarc -w -m wmaps/wmt13.en.wmap --input=output/exp.baseline/LATS/1.fst.gz
222  ...
223  <s> parliament does not support the amendment , which gives you the freedom of tymoshenko </s> 42.6999,-19.4512
224  ...
225 
226  > printstrings.${TGTBINMK}.bin -w -m wmaps/wmt13.en.wmap --input=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG.fst
227  ...
228  <s> parliament does not support the amendment , which gives you the freedom of tymoshenko </s> 42.6999
229  ...
230 
231 **Step 6. (optional)** Remove the pruned LM and apply the full LM. The language model `M/interp.4g.arpa.newstest2012.tune.corenlp.ru.idx.withoptions.mmap` is a heavily pruned version of `M/interp.4g.arpa.newstest2012.tune.corenlp.ru.idx.union.mmap`. **Note** that the larger LM will have to be installed and uncompressed as described in \ref tutorial_install.
232 
233  # remove the pruned LM
234  # since .../1/TG.fst is not lexicographic form, we subtract the pruned lm scores and write a version of the FST with translation scores only
235  > applylm.${TGTBINMK}.bin --lm.load=M/interp.4g.arpa.newstest2012.tune.corenlp.ru.idx.withoptions.mmap --lm.featureweights=-1 --lattice.load=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG.fst --lattice.store=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG-nolm.fst
236  # apply the strong LM
237  > applylm.${TGTBINMK}.bin --lm.load=M/interp.4g.arpa.newstest2012.tune.corenlp.ru.idx.union.mmap --lattice.load=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG-nolm.fst --lattice.store=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG-final.fst
238 
239 
240 The final FSA that results from this process (`output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG-final.fst`) contains translations under the Hiero grammar with the full, unpruned LM:
241 
242  > printstrings.${TGTBINMK}.bin -w -m wmaps/wmt13.en.wmap --input=output/exp.baseline/rtn/1/TG-final.fst
243  ...
244  <s> parliament does not support the amendment , which gives you the freedom of tymoshenko </s> 43.093
245 
246 The overall score changes, due to the bigger LM, but the top hypothesis is unchanged.
